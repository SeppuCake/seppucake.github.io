About the Project

In Malaysia, cyber-related crimes are on the rise. Malaysia's national cybersecurity agency reports 2,100 compromised servers, containing data from government agencies, banks, universities, and businesses. This reflects a lack of awareness and underestimation of cybersecurity. Companies in Malaysia offer network and system security solutions, but the process is complex.

Our project, "Ocelot," addresses the challenges faced by cybersecurity teams during fieldwork. Penetration tests (pen-tests) involve gathering information about a target system or company. We're developing a handheld device, using Linux, Near-Field Communication (NFC), and Android. The device, based on the Nexus 4, aids white hat hackers with security features and penetration testing tools.

Ocelot plays a key role in active reconnaissance, like "Social Engineering." For instance, the device runs NetHunter, an OS based on Kali Linux, which emulates Android with hidden hacking tools. This allows discreet pen-testing in public, under surveillance.

By summarizing, we've highlighted the increasing cybercrime in Malaysia, the need for cybersecurity awareness, and the establishment of companies providing security solutions. We've explained the purpose of the Ocelot project, its components, and its importance in active reconnaissance.

Problem Statement

  1. People tend to neglect the security of a device which leads to unauthorized access:
    • In most cases, having a physical security such as padlocks is merely enough to satisfy the needs of security. However, technology evolves and so does people.
    • Threats such as unsophisticated attacks that could bypass a bad protection practice tend to allow unauthorized access to take its place and bring negative impact on an object, individual or organization.
    • This issue provides hackers or intruders an easy way in-and-outs of a system.
  2. Vulnerabilities pose threat to individuals towards irresponsible individuals such as unethical hackers:
    • Cyber-attacks occur due to exploits of a weakness in a system that gave way for hackers and intruders to gain or steal information in the means for profit or revenge.
    • Vulnerabilities can be exploited in many ways and confidential information can be leaked such as financial documents, operating system manuals, and contacts information.
  3. Most handheld devices are limited to only one Operating System (OS) which limits the usage:
    • Nowadays, smartphones have great features that provide accessibilities for users to perform their daily tasks.
    • Some prefer smartphones as a necessity in this era.
    • Unfortunately, when penetration tests are in place, white hat hackers are not able to fully utilize smartphones as one of their tools to carry out their tasks in discovering vulnerabilities of a network or system.

Project Scope

Innovating the Ocelot

The project scope consists of three key parts:

Part 1: NetHunter Operating System (OS)

  • NetHunter OS provides essential tools from Kali Linux for white hat hackers.
  • Enables efficient penetration testing and vulnerability discovery.

Part 2: Features of NFC Security

  • Utilizes NFC technology in smartphones for secure access to the device.
  • Reduces the risk of unauthorized access and potential threats to the public.
  • Offers a cost-effective alternative to costly RFID-based security systems.

Part 3: Exploitation Using the Device

  • Demonstrates the use of SQL injections and keylogging techniques.
  • Empowers white hat hackers to perform ethical hacking activities.

The Ocelot project aims to enhance the functionality of smartphones for white hat hackers. By incorporating NetHunter OS, it provides a comprehensive suite of tools for effective penetration testing and vulnerability assessment. The inclusion of NFC security features ensures secure access to the device while minimizing the risk of unauthorized access. Furthermore, the project showcases the capabilities of the device through practical demonstrations of SQL injections and keylogging techniques.

With the Ocelot, white hat hackers can discreetly perform their tasks in public without drawing unwanted attention. By bringing together the power of NetHunter OS, NFC security, and exploitation techniques, this project contributes to the advancement of ethical hacking practices and enhances the security landscape.
